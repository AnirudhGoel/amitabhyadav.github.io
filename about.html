About Me

Hi! I am Amitabh Yadav. I am a Master Student in Computer Engineering at TU Delft. Here, I am specializing in Multi-core Processor Architectures, Digital VLSI Design and Quantum Computing. I work on Digital Processor/ASIC Design, FPGA prototyping, and Embedded Software Development. My research interest is in writing firmware and architectures for Computing Platforms and designing scalable Quantum Micro-architectures for (future) Quantum Computers.

I completed my Bachelors in Electronics Engineering from UPES, Dehradun in India. Here, I worked on embedded applications in Early Warning Systems and Space Systems. Upon graduating, I spent my summer working on FPGA prototyping of high-speed communication protocols at CERN, Geneva.

I am a Quantum Computing enthusiast. I study Quantum (Computing) Physics through MOOCs and scientific publications. I study about Quantum Mechanics, Qubit Realization Technologies and Cryogenic-CMOS Technologies.

In my spare time, I enjoy traveling, photography, sketching pencil portraits and playing piano.
